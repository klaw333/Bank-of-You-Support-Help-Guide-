# Bank-of-You-Support-Help-Guide-


Bank of You — Support & Help Guide                                            
                  
  What is Bank of You?

  Bank of You is a personal finance app that helps you manage your discretionary
   spending by simulating a personal bank account. Set a spending limit, track
  expenses, receive recurring allowances, and learn how interest works — all in
  one place. It's perfect for teens, young adults, parents teaching money
  skills, or anyone who wants to stay on top of their spending.

  ---
  Getting Started

  When you first open Bank of You, a default account is created for you. Your
  Dashboard shows your current balance, upcoming deposits, and recent
  transactions. From here you can:

  - Tap Spend to log an expense
  - Tap Deposit to add funds
  - View your Recent Activity at the bottom of the screen

  Your balance shows "In the Green" when positive and "Over Budget" when
  negative.

  ---
  Adding Transactions

  Logging an Expense

  1. Tap the Spend button on the Dashboard.
  2. Enter the amount and a description (e.g., "Coffee" or "New shoes").
  3. Choose the date (defaults to today).
  4. Tap Submit to save.

  Adding a Deposit

  1. Tap the Deposit button on the Dashboard.
  2. Enter the amount and description.
  3. Choose the date and tap Submit.

  Editing or Deleting a Transaction

  Tap any transaction in your Recent Activity list to edit its amount,
  description, date, or type. Use the trash icon to delete it.

  ---
  Recurring Transactions

  Set up automatic, repeating expenses or deposits so you don't have to log them
   manually each time.

  Creating a Recurring Transaction

  1. Tap Recurring Expenses on the Dashboard (or toggle Make Recurring when
  adding a new transaction).
  2. Tap the + button.
  3. Enter the amount, description, and choose a frequency:
    - Daily
    - Weekly (select a day of the week)
    - Bi-Weekly (select a day of the week)
    - Monthly (select a day of the month)
  4. Set a Start Date for when it should begin.
  5. Save your recurring transaction.

  Managing Recurring Transactions

  - Pause/Resume: Swipe on a recurring item to temporarily pause or resume it.
  - Edit: Tap a recurring item to change its amount, frequency, or schedule.
  - Delete: Swipe to remove a recurring transaction entirely.

  ---
  House Rules (Settings)

  Tap the settings icon to access House Rules, where you customize how your
  account works.

  Credit Limit

  Set a maximum amount you're allowed to go into debt. Think of it like a credit
   card limit — you can't spend beyond this amount.

  Allowance (Deposit Schedule)

  Set up automatic deposits that act like a regular allowance or paycheck:
  - Choose the amount you receive each period.
  - Pick a frequency (daily, weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly).
  - Select which day deposits land.
  - Set a start date for when deposits begin.

  Interest

  When your balance goes negative (over budget), the app can charge interest —
  just like a real credit card:
  - Set the interest rate as a percentage.
  - Choose how often interest is charged (daily, weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly).
  - Set a start date for when interest charges begin.

  Interest only applies to negative balances. If you're in the green, you won't
  be charged.

  Appearance

  - Theme: Choose System, Light, or Dark mode.
  - Week Starts On: Set your calendar week to start on Monday or Sunday.
  - Deposit Confetti: Toggle a fun confetti animation when you receive a
  deposit.

  Premium Features

  Bank of You offers a one-time Premium upgrade that unlocks:

  - Unlimited History — Free accounts display up to 30 recent transactions.
  Premium removes this limit.
  - Multiple Accounts — Create up to separate accounts with independent
  balances, settings, and transactions (e.g., "Spending," "Savings," "Fun
  Money").
  - Export to CSV — Download your transaction data as a spreadsheet file to
  share or analyze.
  - Transaction Search — Quickly find past transactions by description, amount,
  or date.

  To upgrade, go to Settings > Upgrade to Premium or tap the crown icon anywhere
   in the app.

  If you've already purchased Premium on another device, tap Restore Purchases
  on the Premium screen.

  ---
  iCloud Sync

  Keep your data backed up and in sync across devices:

  1. Go to Settings > Data Management.
  2. Toggle iCloud Sync on.
  3. Use Sync Now to manually push or pull data.

  Your sync status and last sync time are displayed so you always know your data
   is safe.

  ---
  Exporting Your Data

  (Premium feature)

  1. Go to Settings > Data Management > Export.
  2. Choose All Accounts or a specific account.
  3. Tap Export CSV.
  4. Use the share sheet to save, email, or send your data wherever you need it.

  Frequently Asked Questions

  Q: How is my balance calculated?
  Your balance is the sum of all your deposits minus all your expenses and
  interest charges. It updates in real time as you add or modify transactions.

  Q: What happens when I go over budget?
  Your balance turns negative and shows "Over Budget" in red. If you have
  interest enabled, you'll start accruing charges on that negative balance —
  just like a real credit card.

  Q: Can I use this app for real banking?
  No. Bank of You is a personal budgeting and financial education tool. It does
  not connect to any bank or process real payments.

  Q: Is my data private?
  Yes. All your data is stored locally on your device. If you enable iCloud
  Sync, data is backed up to your personal iCloud account. No data is sent to
  third-party servers.

  Q: I purchased Premium but it's not showing up.
  Go to the Premium screen and tap Restore Purchases. Make sure you're signed in
   with the same Apple ID you used for the original purchase.

  Q: What happens if I delete the app?
  Your data will be lost unless you have iCloud Sync enabled. With iCloud Sync
  on, your data can be restored when you reinstall.

  Q: How do I reset my account?
  Go to Settings > Data Management and use the Clear Data option. This will
  erase all transactions and reset your account. This action cannot be undone.

  Q: What does the interest rate mean?
  The interest rate is the percentage charged on your negative balance each
  period. For example, a 5% rate charged monthly means 5% of your debt is added
  as an interest charge each month.
